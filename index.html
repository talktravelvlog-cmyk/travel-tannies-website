The Story Behind Travel Tannies

From Curious Travelers to Southeast Asia Guides

My husband and I have been traveling through Southeast Asia for more than 15 years. Over that time we’ve explored bustling cities, quiet villages, night markets, temples, and islands. We’ve stayed in everything from simple guest houses to luxury resorts and learned that the best travel moments often happen when you step away from the most crowded tourist spots.

Along the way we made plenty of mistakes, learned a lot, and discovered the places that make this region unforgettable. Travel Tannies exists to help other Gen X travelers experience Southeast Asia with the same sense of curiosity and confidence — without spending months trying to figure everything out on their own.

Places I've Explored

Koh Samui, Thailand

One of our favorite islands in Thailand. We specifically love the **Fisherman's Village in Bophut** for its seaside restaurants and the vibrant night markets that bring the streets to life after sunset. It offers a perfect blend of modern comfort and island charm.

Chiang Mai, Thailand

The cultural heart of the north. We've spent many days exploring the stunning **Wat Phra That Doi Suthep** temple overlooking the city and visiting ethical animal sanctuaries like the **Legend Elephant Sanctuary**. The mountain scenery here is unmatched.

Ha Long Bay & Hanoi, Vietnam

Whether it's navigating the organized chaos of Hanoi's Old Quarter or taking a traditional junk boat through the limestone karst islands of **Ha Long Bay**, Vietnam is a feast for the senses that we return to again and again.

The Perfect 3-Week Southeast Asia Route

Week 1: Temples & Northern Charm

  • Bangkok (3 nights): Explore the Grand Palace, Wat Pho, and the incredible street food scenes.
  • Chiang Mai (4 nights): Visit Doi Suthep at sunset and spend a day at an ethical elephant sanctuary.

Week 2: Vietnam’s Natural Beauty

  • Hanoi (3 nights): Wander the 36 streets of the Old Quarter and try the famous Egg Coffee.
  • Ha Long Bay (2 nights): Overnight cruise through the emerald waters and limestone caves.
  • Hoi An (2 nights): The city of lanterns and world-class tailors.

Week 3: Bali Island Bliss

  • Ubud (4 nights): Rice terraces, sacred temples, and local artisan markets.
  • Uluwatu (3 nights): Sunset at the cliffside temple and relaxing at world-famous beach clubs.
