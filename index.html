2 Weeks in Bali: The Perfect First‑Time Itinerary

Bali is one of the most popular destinations in Southeast Asia. With lush jungles, incredible temples, beach clubs and island adventures, it’s a dream destination for first‑time travelers.

If you'd prefer expert help designing your trip, you can book a private travel planning session where we build your itinerary together.

Days 1–4: Ubud – Culture and Jungle

Start in Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. Explore rice terraces, temples and traditional Balinese culture.

  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Sacred Monkey Forest
  • Balinese cooking class
  • Ubud Palace cultural dance

Days 5–7: Mount Batur Adventure

Wake early for a sunrise hike up Mount Batur. It’s one of the most memorable experiences in Bali.

Days 8–10: Uluwatu Beaches

Head south for cliffside beaches and sunset views.

  • Padang Padang Beach
  • Uluwatu Temple sunset
  • Beach clubs like Sundays and Savaya

Days 11–14: Nusa Islands

Finish your trip exploring Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

  • Kelingking Beach viewpoint
  • Angel’s Billabong
  • Manta ray snorkelling

Thailand Island Hopping Guide

Thailand’s islands are famous for turquoise water, limestone cliffs and vibrant beach towns.

Phuket

The largest island and a great starting point for exploring southern Thailand.

Krabi

Known for dramatic cliffs and stunning beaches like Railay Beach.

Phi Phi Islands

One of the most famous island groups in Southeast Asia.

Koh Samui

A relaxed island with luxury resorts, waterfalls and temples.

First Time in Vietnam – Complete Travel Guide

Vietnam is one of Southeast Asia’s most exciting destinations, offering incredible food, dramatic landscapes and rich history.

Hanoi

Vietnam’s capital is full of culture, street food and colonial architecture.

Ha Long Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage site famous for limestone islands rising from emerald water.

Hoi An

A beautiful lantern‑lit town known for tailoring and historic streets.

Ho Chi Minh City

A vibrant modern city full of markets, rooftop bars and museums.
