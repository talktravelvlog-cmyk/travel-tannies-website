Mistakes We Made (So You Don't Have To)

Trying to See Too Many Places

In our early trips, we tried to cram too many destinations into a short time. We learned that travel days often disappear between flights, boat transfers, and check‑ins. Now we focus on slower travel that lets you actually enjoy where you are.

Booking the Wrong Areas

Where you stay can completely change your experience. We’ve stayed in places that were too loud, too far from the action, or just didn't match the vibe we were looking for. We now know which areas in Bali, Thailand, and Vietnam offer the best balance of convenience and atmosphere.

Not Checking Visa Rules Early

Visa requirements for South Africans (and many other nationalities) can be tricky and change often. We’ve learned to check these months in advance to avoid last‑minute stress at the airport.

Places I've Explored

Koh Samui, Thailand

One of our favorite islands. We love the Fisherman's Village in Bophut for seaside dining and the vibrant night markets that bring the streets to life after sunset.

Chiang Mai, Thailand

The cultural heart. From the stunning Doi Suthep temple overlooking the city to ethical sanctuaries like the Legend Elephant Sanctuary, Chiang Mai always feels special.

Ha Long Bay & Hanoi, Vietnam

Vietnam is a feast for the senses. Whether it's the organized chaos of Hanoi's Old Quarter or a quiet boat cruise through Ha Long Bay, it's a country that stays with you.

The Perfect 3-Week Southeast Asia Route

Week 1: Bangkok & Northern Culture

  • Bangkok (3 nights): Riverside temples, street food, and the Grand Palace.
  • Chiang Mai (4 nights): Mountain temples, Old City charm, and elephant sanctuaries.

Week 2: Vietnam Highlights

  • Hanoi (3 nights): The Old Quarter, coffee culture, and history.
  • Ha Long Bay (2 nights): Overnight cruise through limestone karsts.
  • Hoi An (2 nights): Lantern-lit streets and ancient architecture.

Week 3: Bali Island Life

  • Ubud (4 nights): Rice terraces, sacred temples, and monkey forest.
  • Uluwatu (3 nights): Cliffside temples, surf culture, and sunset beach clubs.

Featured Guide: 2 Weeks in Bali

Days 1-5: Ubud Culture

Immerse yourself in Balinese culture, explore the Tegalalang Rice Terraces, and visit sacred temples like Tirta Empul.

Days 6-10: North Bali & Volcanoes

Hike Mount Batur at sunrise, visit the Ulun Danu Beratan temple, and explore the quiet beaches of Amed.

Days 11-14: Island Relaxation

Spend your final days on the Nusa Islands or enjoying the cliffside beach clubs of Uluwatu in the south.
