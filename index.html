Mistakes We Made in Southeast Asia (So You Don't Have To)

When my husband and I first started traveling through Southeast Asia more than 15 years ago, we made plenty of mistakes. Some were small, some cost us time, and some meant we missed incredible experiences. The good news is that you don't have to learn everything the hard way.

Trying to See Too Many Places

On our early trips we tried to squeeze too many destinations into one itinerary. Southeast Asia looks close together on a map, but travel days can quickly disappear between flights, ferries and long transfers.

Booking the Wrong Area

The first time we visited some destinations we booked accommodation in the wrong part of town simply because we didn't understand the layout. Where you stay can completely change your experience.

Underestimating Travel Times

What looks like a short journey can easily become a full travel day when you add airport transfers, delays and connections. Now we always build breathing room into our itineraries.

Missing Incredible Experiences

Some of the most memorable experiences we discovered later were things we didn't even know existed on our first trips — hidden temples, night markets, local villages and ethical elephant sanctuaries.

Packing the Wrong Things

We quickly learned that Southeast Asia travel requires light clothing, good walking shoes, mosquito protection and practical travel gear.

Not Understanding Visa Rules Early

Visa rules vary between countries. Some require e‑visas, others allow visa‑free entry. Checking requirements early prevents stress before your trip.

Travel Tannies exists so you can enjoy the magic of Southeast Asia without having to learn everything the hard way like we did.

This photo was taken at the famous Wat Phra That Doi Suthep temple in Chiang Mai, one of the most iconic temples in northern Thailand. The temple sits high on a mountain overlooking the city and is known for its stunning golden stupa.

This photo was taken at the beautiful Legend Elephant Sanctuary in Chiang Mai. Northern Thailand is famous for ethical elephant sanctuaries where visitors can observe and interact with rescued elephants in a responsible way.

Experiences like this are often highlights of a Thailand itinerary. Many travelers combine Chiang Mai's temples, mountain scenery and elephant sanctuaries with island destinations like Phuket or Koh Samui.

This photo was taken during a cruise through the spectacular Ha Long Bay in northern Vietnam, one of Southeast Asia’s most famous natural wonders and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Thousands of limestone karst islands rise from emerald water, creating one of the most dramatic landscapes in the world. Most travelers visit Ha Long Bay from Hanoi and spend one or two nights on a traditional cruise boat.

2 Weeks in Bali: The Perfect First‑Time Itinerary

Bali is one of the most popular destinations in Southeast Asia. With lush jungles, incredible temples, beach clubs and island adventures, it’s a dream destination for first‑time travelers.

If you'd prefer expert help designing your trip, you can book a private travel planning session where we build your itinerary together.

Days 1–4: Ubud – Culture and Jungle

Start in Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. Explore rice terraces, temples and traditional Balinese culture.

  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Sacred Monkey Forest
  • Balinese cooking class
  • Ubud Palace cultural dance

Days 5–7: Mount Batur Adventure

Wake early for a sunrise hike up Mount Batur. It’s one of the most memorable experiences in Bali.

Days 8–10: Uluwatu Beaches

Head south for cliffside beaches and sunset views.

  • Padang Padang Beach
  • Uluwatu Temple sunset
  • Beach clubs like Sundays and Savaya

Days 11–14: Nusa Islands

Finish your trip exploring Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

  • Kelingking Beach viewpoint
  • Angel’s Billabong
  • Manta ray snorkelling

3 Week Southeast Asia Itinerary for First-Time Travelers

If you're visiting Southeast Asia for the first time, three weeks is the perfect amount of time to experience multiple countries without feeling rushed. This itinerary combines vibrant cities, cultural landmarks, tropical islands and unforgettable food experiences.

If you'd like help customizing this route to match your travel style, you can book a private travel planning session and receive a personalized itinerary.

Week 1: Thailand – Bangkok & Chiang Mai

Start your journey in Thailand, one of the easiest and most welcoming countries for first-time Southeast Asia travelers.

  • Explore Bangkok’s temples and floating markets
  • Visit the Grand Palace and Wat Pho
  • Fly to Chiang Mai for temples, night markets and mountain scenery
  • Visit an ethical elephant sanctuary

Week 2: Vietnam – Hanoi & Ha Long Bay

Vietnam offers incredible food, dramatic landscapes and fascinating history.

  • Explore Hanoi’s Old Quarter and street food scene
  • Take a 1–2 night cruise through Ha Long Bay
  • Visit traditional villages and limestone islands

Week 3: Bali – Culture & Beaches

Finish your trip in Bali where you can relax and explore temples, rice terraces and beaches.

  • Stay in Ubud for culture and jungle scenery
  • Visit the Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Watch sunset at Uluwatu Temple
  • Take a day trip to the Nusa Islands

This 3‑week route gives travelers a balanced introduction to Southeast Asia with culture, nature, beaches and world‑class food.

Frequently Asked Questions About Southeast Asia Travel

When is the best time to visit Southeast Asia?

The best time to visit most of Southeast Asia is during the dry season between November and April. Weather is generally sunny with lower humidity, making it ideal for island hopping and sightseeing.

How long should I spend in Southeast Asia?

Most travelers spend 2–3 weeks exploring one or two countries. This allows enough time to experience cities, beaches and cultural sites without rushing.

Is Southeast Asia safe for first‑time travelers?

Yes. Countries like Thailand, Bali (Indonesia), Vietnam and Singapore are very popular with first‑time travelers. With basic precautions and good planning, travel in the region is generally safe and welcoming.

Do I need visas for Southeast Asia?

Visa requirements vary by country and nationality. Thailand and Indonesia often allow visa‑free entry for many travelers, while Vietnam may require an e‑visa. Always check official government sources before travel.

How do I plan a multi‑country Southeast Asia trip?

Start by choosing two or three destinations, then plan flights and routes between them. If you'd like expert help building the perfect itinerary, you can book a travel planning consultation.
