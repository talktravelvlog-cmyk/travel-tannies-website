This photo was taken at the famous Wat Phra That Doi Suthep temple in Chiang Mai, one of the most iconic temples in northern Thailand. The temple sits high on a mountain overlooking the city and is known for its stunning golden stupa.

This photo was taken during a cruise through the spectacular Ha Long Bay in northern Vietnam, one of Southeast Asia’s most famous natural wonders and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2 Weeks in Bali: The Perfect First‑Time Itinerary

Bali is one of the most popular destinations in Southeast Asia. With lush jungles, incredible temples, beach clubs and island adventures, it’s a dream destination for first‑time travelers.

If you'd prefer expert help designing your trip, you can book a private travel planning session where we build your itinerary together.

Days 1–4: Ubud – Culture and Jungle

Start in Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. Explore rice terraces, temples and traditional Balinese culture.

  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Sacred Monkey Forest
  • Balinese cooking class
  • Ubud Palace cultural dance

Days 5–7: Mount Batur Adventure

Wake early for a sunrise hike up Mount Batur. It’s one of the most memorable experiences in Bali.

Days 8–10: Uluwatu Beaches

Head south for cliffside beaches and sunset views.

  • Padang Padang Beach
  • Uluwatu Temple sunset
  • Beach clubs like Sundays and Savaya

Days 11–14: Nusa Islands

Finish your trip exploring Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

  • Kelingking Beach viewpoint
  • Angel’s Billabong
  • Manta ray snorkelling

Frequently Asked Questions About Southeast Asia Travel

When is the best time to visit Southeast Asia?

The best time to visit most of Southeast Asia is during the dry season between November and April. Weather is generally sunny with lower humidity, making it ideal for island hopping and sightseeing.

How long should I spend in Southeast Asia?

Most travelers spend 2–3 weeks exploring one or two countries. This allows enough time to experience cities, beaches and cultural sites without rushing.

Is Southeast Asia safe for first‑time travelers?

Yes. Countries like Thailand, Bali (Indonesia), Vietnam and Singapore are very popular with first‑time travelers. With basic precautions and good planning, travel in the region is generally safe and welcoming.

Do I need visas for Southeast Asia?

Visa requirements vary by country and nationality. Thailand and Indonesia often allow visa‑free entry for many travelers, while Vietnam may require an e‑visa. Always check official government sources before travel.

How do I plan a multi‑country Southeast Asia trip?

Start by choosing two or three destinations, then plan flights and routes between them. If you'd like expert help building the perfect itinerary, you can book a travel planning consultation.
