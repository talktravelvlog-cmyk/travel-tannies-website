The Story Behind Travel Tannies

From Curious Travelers to Southeast Asia Guides

My husband and I have been traveling through Southeast Asia for more than 15 years. Over that time we’ve explored bustling cities, quiet villages, night markets, and places most first‑time visitors don’t even know exist.

Along the way we made plenty of mistakes, learned a lot, and discovered the places that make this region unforgettable. Travel Tannies exists to help other Gen X travelers experience Southeast Asia with the same sense of curiosity and confidence.

Mistakes We Made (So You Don't Have To)

Trying to See Too Many Places

In our early trips, we tried to cram too many destinations into a short time. Travel days disappear between flights, boat transfers, and check‑ins. Now we focus on slower travel that lets you actually enjoy where you are.

Booking the Wrong Areas

Where you stay changes your experience. We now know which areas in Bali, Thailand, and Vietnam offer the best balance of convenience and atmosphere.

Not Checking Visa Rules Early

Visa requirements for South Africans can be tricky. We’ve learned to check these months in advance to avoid last‑minute stress at the airport.

The Perfect 2-Week Bali Itinerary

Days 1-5: Ubud

The cultural heart. Rice terraces, sacred temples, and local artisan markets.

Days 6-10: North Bali

Mount Batur sunrise trekking and the quiet beaches of Amed.

Days 11-14: Islands & Beach

Explore the Nusa Islands or the cliffside sunset at Uluwatu Temple.

3-Week Southeast Asia Route

Week 1: Thailand - Start in Bangkok for temples and food, then head to Chiang Mai for culture.

Week 2: Vietnam - Hanoi's Old Quarter, Ha Long Bay Cruise, and the lanterns of Hoi An.

Week 3: Bali - Finish with spiritual Ubud and world-famous beaches in the south.
